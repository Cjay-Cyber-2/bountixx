# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Competitive individuals aged 16-35 who want to settle debates, test skills, and earn bragging rights through structured head-to-head challenges. They arrive from a desktop, on evenings and weekends, already primed for competition. They know gaming, they know wagering, they're not afraid of a UI with density and edge.

## Product Purpose

Bountixx is an AI-structured multiplayer challenge arena. Anyone can drop a raw challenge (code problem, trivia, logic puzzle, math sprint) and the AI builds the room: classifies the challenge type, sets difficulty, structures test cases. Rivals fill slots, enter simultaneously, and a deterministic judgment engine crowns a single winner who claims the coin bounty. First 10 arenas are free; coins drive the economy from there.

## Brand Personality

Ruthless · Kinetic · Underground

The brand feels like a late-night hackathon hosted in a brutalist server room. It's tense, electric, and specific — not game-ified-for-everyone, but built for people who want real stakes.

## Anti-references

- Sanitized gamification platforms (Kahoot, Quizlet): too friendly, too pastel, no stakes
- Generic SaaS purple (Linear, Notion, Vercel): the void purple accent is fine but the "modern startup clean" layout approach is wrong for this brand
- LeetCode / HackerRank: functional-ugly, no competitive identity, no economy
- The cream/warm-neutral AI landing page trend of 2025-2026: completely wrong register

## Design Principles

1. **Stakes are visible.** Every surface communicates that something real can be won or lost. Don't soften or abstract the competitive edge.
2. **Density signals competence.** The audience can handle information-dense UIs. Breathing room is earned, not default. Don't pad things into vagueness.
3. **Dark is structural, light is contrast.** The dark mode is the native environment. Light mode is a high-contrast inversion — pure white, not warm, with the same brand voice.
4. **One winner per section.** Each section of the landing page makes one argument. Headings don't restate the eyebrow; content doesn't repeat the heading.
5. **Type carries the brand.** Zen Dots for titles, Rajdhani for body, Space Mono for data/labels, Orbitron for UI chrome. This stack is committed; it doesn't need gradient text to add personality.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Body text and placeholders must hit 4.5:1 contrast on their background. Both dark and light modes must independently pass contrast. Reduce-motion alternative for all animations.
